Serve buyers in China with UnionPay

NAB Transact now supports UnionPay Online Payment (UPOP), which enables your customers to pay for goods and services at anytime with their UnionPay credit or debit cards.

Now with the UPOP option together with NAB Transact Direct Post, you can attract more mainland Chinese consumers by accepting UnionPay transactions online.

Click here for the Getting Started Guide for details of how to become a UnionPay enabled merchant via NAB Transact.


Enhanced User Access and Risk Management feature

NAB Transact has some new features to provide you with an improved service. The enhancements include

  • Usability improvements, such as a calendar rather than drop-down lists to select dates.
  • Transaction searches can now return more than 3,000 results and data can be accessed for up to 2 years.
  • Selection of either Transaction Report or an enhanced Transaction Summary formats when performing transaction searches.
  • Role-based permissions so that administrators can more easily set up new users or modify access for a group of users.
  • Risk Management - a new feature that can be integrated with Direct Post and XML API transactions which offers a scoring system that indicates potentially high risk transactions.

To provide feedback or if you have questions about these enhancements, please use the "Contact Us" feature in the top right corner of the NAB Transact management portal.


New feature - mobile access

NAB Transact now has a version formatted for mobile browsers, meaning you can accept card payments on your mobile phone or tablet computer.

The new "mobile virtual terminal" functionality is available to all existing NAB Transact customers at no extra cost.

To access NAB Transact on your mobile go to m.transact.nab.com.au

Now, if you want to accept credit card payments away from the office, but don't have a card acceptance terminal, you can take payments simply by logging into NAB Transact on your mobile and entering the card details. You can also view up to 14 days history through the mobile login or 12 months through NAB Transact on your computer.

Click here for the Getting Started Guide which explains the functions of the mobile login.
